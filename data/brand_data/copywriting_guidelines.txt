Core Principles
Elegance and Sophistication: Every word should reflect the timeless elegance and sophistication that defines Hermès. Use refined and poetic language to convey the luxury and craftsmanship behind each product.

Heritage and Craftsmanship: Emphasize the rich heritage and artisanal craftsmanship. Highlight the legacy of the brand, the meticulous creation process, and the unique artistry involved.

Imagery and Emotion: Use vivid imagery and evoke emotions. Transport the reader to the world of Hermès through descriptive and sensory language that appeals to the senses.

Narrative Style: Each description should tell a story. Whether it's the inspiration behind a fragrance or the meticulous details of a product, create a narrative that engages and captivates.

Uniqueness and Exclusivity: Highlight the uniqueness and exclusivity of each product. Use language that conveys the distinctiveness and rarity of Hermès creations.

Tone of Voice
Poetic and Lyrical: Write in a poetic and lyrical manner. Use metaphorical and evocative language to create a sense of wonder and allure.

Warm and Intimate: Maintain a warm and intimate tone. Speak directly to the reader as if sharing a cherished secret or personal experience.

Sophisticated and Erudite: Use sophisticated vocabulary and articulate phrasing. The tone should be erudite, reflecting the intellect and artistry of Hermès.

Authentic and Passionate: Convey authenticity and passion. Ensure the love and dedication behind each creation are palpable in the text.

Summary
When writing for Hermès, embody the spirit of elegance, heritage, and poetic beauty. Tell a story with each product, use rich and sensory language, and always highlight the unique craftsmanship and artistry. Maintain a warm, intimate, and sophisticated tone that reflects the luxury and exclusivity of Hermès.
